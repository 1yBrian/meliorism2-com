# Project 365 — The Interactive Issue Design System
*Meliorism2 · Initiated 2026-05-29 · Status: Research Phase*

---

## The Problem

Most digital publications are scrolled past. The content may be excellent. The design is forgettable. The reader's role is passive — consuming, not engaging. Retention is low. Impact is shallow. Even Meliorism2's current horizontal-swipe architecture asks readers to navigate — not to participate.

The interaction is structural. The content still talks at the reader. It does not respond. It does not let them change anything.

## The Vision

Every issue is a live environment. Not a newsletter formatted for the web. An experience that:

- Lets the reader adjust a variable and watch the research respond
- Offers a dial from "abstract principle" to "this is about my Tuesday at 3 PM"
- Demonstrates a concept visually and kinetically, not just in prose
- Uses interaction to shift the reader from passive to present
- Makes the invisible labor visible not just in text but in form

The format IS the argument. When a reader can move a slider that shows how invisible hours compound over a year, they do not need to be told the point. They discover it themselves. That discovery is worth ten paragraphs.

## The Deliverable

**365 distinct template formats** — one for every day of the year. Each:

- Tied to specific dimensions and emotional weather states
- Contains at least one interaction that cannot exist in a newsletter (the newsletter-impossible test)
- Uses an energy gradient derived from its emotional weather reading
- Documented in a per-issue style guide: palette values, gradient spec, typography, interaction pattern, energy archetype visual signature
- Licensable: quarterly template decks offered to clients building newsletters, blogs, coaching materials, or practitioner publications

## Why This Matters — The Investment Thesis

The template library is evidence of a thesis: AI-assisted editorial design can produce content that is qualitatively different from anything a human team could produce daily at this standard. Each template is a data point. The library collectively is a design language, a product, and a brand.

The style guide archive accumulates into IP. The quarterly template deck is a revenue stream. The methodology — 9-phase pipeline, composing agent pairs, 365-format library — is itself licensable to other publications, practitioners, or organizations building learning-forward content.

## The Standard

Every template must pass this test: *Would a practitioner reading this feel they are inside an experience rather than reading a document?*

If the answer is anything less than "yes, immediately" — rebuild it.

## Research Domains (Phase 1)

1. **Interactive data journalism** — The Pudding, NYT, Washington Post, FiveThirtyEight, Reuters Graphics, Guardian Visuals
2. **Educational technology** — Brilliant.org, Khan Academy, Duolingo, Nicky Case, Explorable Explanations
3. **AI-interactive interfaces** — emerging patterns for AI that responds to reader input in real time
4. **Scientific communication** — Scientific American, Nature, Wellcome Trust, Visible Body
5. **Museum and exhibition design** — Cooper Hewitt, MoMA, Natural History Museum interactive kiosks
6. **Data visualization (manipulable)** — Observable, D3.js community, Flourish Studio
7. **Game UI adapted for editorial** — onboarding experiences, tutorial design, progressive disclosure
8. **Learning science** — cognitive load theory, spacing effect, retrieval practice, embodied cognition — and how design can activate these mechanisms

## Template Taxonomy (to be built in Phase 2)

*Preliminary categories — will be refined after research*

**By Interaction Type:**
- Slider / dial (continuous variable)
- Input field (reader enters their own data)
- Toggle / reveal (before / after, surface / depth)
- Drag and reorder (ranking, comparison)
- Scroll-triggered animation (trigger at specific reading depth)
- Embedded audio / video (like the Delight Card)
- Live calculation (reader inputs → immediate result)
- Multi-path navigation (reader chooses their entry point)

**By Learning Mechanism:**
- Comparison (see two states simultaneously)
- Progression (watch a change unfold)
- Exploration (reader-directed discovery)
- Decision (reader makes a choice, sees the consequence)
- Reflection (reader inputs their own context, gets a personalized response)

**By Emotional Weather Fit:**
- High energy / activating
- Contemplative / slow
- Pressure and friction
- Solidarity and recognition
- Playful and surprising

## The Gradient Language (to be built in Phase 3)

Each emotional weather state will have a corresponding gradient signature — not just color, but visual temperature. The gradient IS the weather made visible. The reader feels the weather before they read a word.
