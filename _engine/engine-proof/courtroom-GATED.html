Ruling

The razor is a direction, not a destination.

Begin where the assumptions are fewest. Not because the simple answer is always right — but because it is where you must look first, and what you must rule out before you reach for the elaborate one. The razor tells you where to start cutting. It never tells you when the wood runs out.

Etymology

parsimony — from Latin parsimonia, "sparingness," from parcere, to spare. And razor: the blade that shaves away — not what is false, but what is unnecessary. Ockham never wrote of a razor. The metaphor was sharpened onto his name centuries later.

Bibliography
William of Ockham. Summa Logicae & Quodlibeta Septem (c. 1323). The principle in his own words: "Numquam ponenda est pluralitas sine necessitate" — plurality must never be posited without necessity.
John Punch (Johannes Poncius). Commentary on Duns Scotus, 1639. Source of the famous phrasing "Entia non sunt multiplicanda praeter necessitatem" — the line everyone quotes as Ockham's, written three centuries after his death.
Sober, Elliott. Ockham's Razors: A User's Manual. Cambridge University Press, 2015. On when parsimony is a guide to truth and when it is merely a guide to where to look.
Akaike, Hirotugu. "A New Look at the Statistical Model Identification." IEEE Transactions on Automatic Control, 1974. The razor made mathematical: a criterion that penalizes added assumptions — formal proof that simpler models often predict better.
Walter of Chatton. Ockham's contemporary, who proposed an anti-razor: if a few things do not suffice, posit more. The dissent that keeps the razor honest.

Sea otters hold hands while they sleep.

Not for warmth. For anchorage. Adrift on open water, an otter can wake to find the current has carried it far from everyone it knows. So before they sleep, they reach out and take the paw of another — sometimes a whole raft of them, linked, drifting together so none drifts alone.

Pups who haven't learned the habit are sometimes wrapped in giant kelp by their mothers — moored to the seabed like a small boat — so they stay put while she dives.

No mechanism. No lesson. Just something true and good, because you walked all the way here.
