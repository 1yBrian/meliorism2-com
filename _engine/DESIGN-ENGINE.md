# THE DESIGN ENGINE
## Meliorism2 · The system that designs unique immersive templates — fast, forever
*Initiated 2026-05-29 · This is the product behind the product.*

---

## THE REFRAME

We are not building 365 templates. A library runs out. A library repeats.

We are building **the engine that generates a unique, immersive, theme-true design from any input — infinitely.** The 365 issues this year are not 365 designs we found. They are 365 *outputs* of one generative system, each with a different hue, vibe, structure, and way to interact.

The test of success is not "is this the best template." The test is: **can the engine produce an excellent, never-before-seen design in minutes, from any concept, every single day, without repeating itself or dropping quality?**

That engine is the second product. A teacher points it at any lesson — fractions, the water cycle, the causes of a war — and it generates an interactive experience that makes students *do* the idea instead of read it. The target: 50%+ engagement lift, because the mechanisms are not decoration. They are how the brain encodes.

---

## THE CORE PRINCIPLE — SHOW, DON'T TELL

Every design the engine produces obeys one law:

> **The reader discovers the point by doing something. The interaction IS the thesis.**

A newsletter tells you teachers underestimate their hours. The engine builds a clock that runs your unbilled time up in front of you until it lands as loss. The fact is the same. The encoding is not. One is read and forgotten. One is *done* and kept.

This is the difference between a document and a space. A document is delivered to you. A space is entered by you. Everything the engine makes is a space.

---

## THE LAW OF EARNED ADVANCE  *(non-negotiable · the structural test)*

> **You cannot scroll to the next piece of information. You must DO something to reach it. The gesture that advances IS the interaction.**

A newsletter scrolls. Information is free — the reader slides past it, skims, leaves. The interaction, if any, is a widget bolted onto a document the reader could ignore entirely.

A space **gates**. The next idea is *earned*, not delivered. To reach the verdict, you must rank the theories. To see the cleared X-ray, you must peel the films. To find the sweet spot, you must move the dial there yourself. The information is the *reward* for the engagement — never the default state.

**The structural test, applied to every generated issue:**
- Could the reader reach the end by scrolling and never touching anything? → **FAIL. Rebuild.**
- Is there a "next" arrow they can mash without engaging? → **FAIL.** The advance gesture must require the interaction's real input.
- Does each card's content unlock only after its micro-action is completed? → **PASS.**

This is why `IntersectionObserver` reveal-on-scroll and `scrollIntoView` navigation are **forbidden as the primary progression mechanism.** They reproduce the newsletter. Scroll is for re-reading what you've already earned — never for first discovery.

The engine-proof run (3 Occam variants, 2026-05-29) violated this — all three scrolled like newsletters with widgets attached. That failure is what produced this law. Every issue from here forward gates progression behind the doing.

---

## THE LAW OF REAL DELIGHT  *(the gift must actually delight)*

The Delight Card is not clever trivia. "Ockham never wrote the razor" is intellectually neat — and it is not delight. Delight makes someone **sigh with appreciation, laugh quietly, or want to go somewhere.** A footnote about authorship does none of those.

And under Earned Advance, the delight is the **final reward** — revealed only when the reader has done the work to reach it. It is never scrolled-past. It is the thing waiting at the end of the space, opened like a gift, because they earned it.

Test: would a tired practitioner at the end of this issue feel *given to*? Not informed — *given to*. If not, it is not delight. Rebuild it.

## THE FOUR GENERATIVE LAYERS

Uniqueness is combinatorial. Four layers, each chosen fresh per issue, multiply into an effectively infinite design space. No two issues land on the same coordinates.

### LAYER 1 — ATMOSPHERE  *(the feel · the hue)*
Derived from **emotional weather × energy archetype.**

- **Weather** sets the gradient, color temperature, and light. (7 states — see palette below.)
- **Archetype** sets typographic register, spacing rhythm, and motion character. (7+ voices, growing weekly.)

7 weathers × 7 archetypes = **49 base atmospheres** before a single other choice. Each feels distinct on arrival — before the reader reads a word, the hue has already told them what kind of room they entered.

### LAYER 2 — CONTAINER  *(the world · the vibe)*
Not a layout. A **conceptual space the issue physically inhabits.** The container is chosen by asking: *what real-world object or place naturally holds this idea?*

A barograph. A surgical theater. A ledger. A tide chart. A control tower. A field survey. A confessional booth. A legislative chamber. An optician's chart. A provenance document.

The container dictates the spatial grammar — how cards are framed, how navigation feels, what the metaphor of "moving through" is. The reader is never "on page 3." They are deeper into the *room*. The container is generated per issue and is **never reused identically** — the same metaphor may return in a new decade, a new material, a new scale.

### LAYER 3 — INTERACTION  *(the verb · the way to engage)*
One newsletter-impossible element per issue, selected from the primitive set (below) by asking: *what would let the reader DO this concept rather than be told it?*

The interaction is not a feature bolted on. It is the load-bearing wall. Remove it and the issue collapses into prose. This is the layer that produces the 50%+ engagement lift — because it recruits prediction, embodiment, consequence, and feedback, which are the actual mechanics of encoding.

### LAYER 4 — DELIGHT  *(the gift · the reason to finish)*
Always earned, always off-brief. A video. A poem. A map. A strange fact. A 1963 Volkswagen crossing the Sahara. The reader walked through the craft to get here. This is waiting for them. It has no mechanism and no agenda. It is the proof the publication sees them as a person, not a professional.

**The math:** 49 atmospheres × ∞ containers × 15+ interactions × ∞ delights. The recipe book never closes. The engine cannot run out.

## THE GENERATION PROTOCOL — FAST IS THE POINT
*How an agent produces a complete, unique design spec in minutes, not hours.*

**Input contract** (what the engine receives):
- `concept` — the one idea this issue/lesson delivers
- `emotional_goal` — how the reader should feel (maps to weather)
- `desired_action` — what the reader should DO or understand differently
- `dimension` / `subject` — the domain (practitioner dimension, or for classroom: the subject)
- `audience` — practitioner | student-grade-band | general

**The engine runs five steps:**

1. **Set Atmosphere** — map emotional_goal → weather → gradient + accent. Pull archetype from editorial decision (or pick the one whose voice fits the concept). *Output: hue + type register.*
2. **Find the Container** — ask "what object or place naturally holds this idea?" Generate 3 candidate metaphors. Pick the one with the richest spatial grammar that has not been used recently. *Output: the world.*
3. **Choose the Interaction** — ask "what would make the reader DO this?" Match concept to a primitive. If no primitive fits cleanly, invent one and log it. *Output: the verb.*
4. **Compose the Spec** — atmosphere + container + interaction → a complete design specification: palette, type, card grammar, the one interactive element fully specified, the delight. *Output: build-ready spec.*
5. **Write the Style Guide** — every generation emits `YYYY-MM-DD-[slug]-style-guide.md`. This is what accumulates into the licensable library and the quarterly investor/template deck.

**Speed target:** spec in under 5 minutes of agent time. Build in under an hour. The engine is judged on throughput × uniqueness × quality — not on perfecting one template.

**HARD TYPOGRAPHY FLOOR (non-negotiable, every output):** No font-size below **0.8rem** anywhere in any generated file. The only defensible exception is SVG-internal axis tick labels. This is a standing Brian standard — the engine-proof run violated it across all three variants (down to 0.6rem) and it had to be fixed after the fact. Bake it in: every generation runs a final pass bumping any sub-0.8rem font-size to 0.8rem before the file is considered done. Mobile body text stays at the 1rem (≈18px) floor from PUBLICATION-SOUL.

## THE UNIQUENESS GUARANTEE
*How the engine never repeats across 365 issues.*

Before finalizing, the engine checks the **Design Ledger** (`_engine/DESIGN-LEDGER.md` — one row per published issue: weather, archetype, container, primitive, dominant hue). It enforces:

- **No identical (container × primitive) pair** ever repeats.
- **No same primary hue** within a rolling 7-issue window.
- **No same container family** within a rolling 14-issue window.
- **No same archetype** as the immediately prior issue.

If a generated spec collides, the engine regenerates the colliding layer. The ledger is the memory that makes 365 genuinely different feels possible — and provable to an investor.

## THE CLASSROOM PRODUCT  *(the second product)*

The engine, pointed at education, is an **engagement system**:

- Teacher inputs: `concept` (the lesson) + `emotional_goal` (curiosity, awe, urgency) + `desired_action` (what students should be able to do).
- Engine outputs: a container + interaction + visual direction. The teacher is not a designer. The engine is the designer.
- The interaction primitives are chosen for the documented +50% engagement mechanisms — prediction error, embodiment, consequence, personal relevance.

**The pitch:** every other ed-tech tool gives teachers *content*. This gives them *experiences* — generated fresh per lesson, never the same twice, each one built on how learning actually encodes. A water-cycle lesson becomes a dial the student turns to flood a valley. A fractions lesson becomes a pizza the student cuts and defends. The teacher brings a generated space, not a worksheet.

**Status:** product concept. Scope and go-to-market are an open decision (expansion of Meliorism2 vs. standalone offering). Logged for a dedicated session.
